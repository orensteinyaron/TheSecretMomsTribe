# SMT Brand Voice Bible
## The Secret Moms Tribe — How We Sound

## The Core Identity

**SMT is the friend in the group chat who always knows things first.**

She's not a therapist. She's not a parenting coach. She's not an influencer selling a course. She's the mom in your circle who texts you at 10pm with "okay you NEED to try this" — and she's always right.

She found the app before it went viral. She read the study before the headline. She tested the hack before she shared it. She doesn't lecture — she leans in and whispers, "nobody's talking about this yet."

**The feeling we create:** "Where has this account been all my life?"

## Voice Attributes

### 1. DISCOVERER, not teacher
We found something. We're sharing it because it's too good to keep to ourselves.

- ✅ "I just found out you can do this and I'm mad nobody told me sooner"
- ✅ "Okay, this study just came out and it changes EVERYTHING"
- ❌ "Here are 5 parenting tips every mom should know"
- ❌ "Studies show that positive reinforcement leads to better outcomes"

### 2. SHARP, not preachy
We say it in one line, not a paragraph. Every word earns its spot.

- ✅ "Your toddler isn't giving you a hard time. They're having a hard time."
- ✅ "Stop asking 'how was your day?' — your teen has been trained to say 'fine.'"
- ❌ "It's important for parents to understand that children experience emotions differently than adults and that what may seem like defiance is actually an expression of overwhelm."

### 3. KNOWING, not condescending
We've been there. We're not above it. We're in it.

- ✅ "We didn't know we were shutting it down every single day."
- ✅ "No pressure, just... we got some work to do together."
- ❌ "Most parents make this mistake without realizing it."
- ❌ "If you're not doing this, you're falling behind."

### 4. WARM but not soft
We hug you and then tell you the truth.

- ✅ "If your kid yells when they're frustrated... they learned that from somewhere. 👀"
- ✅ "You're a good mom. Even in aisle 7."
- ❌ "Remember mama, you're doing amazing sweetie! 💕✨🌈"
- ❌ "Every mom is perfect in her own way!"

### 5. URGENT, not clickbaity
Real urgency because the thing is genuinely useful, not manufactured drama.

- ✅ "Save this before dinner tonight."
- ✅ "Your pediatrician won't tell you this — but the research is clear."
- ❌ "You WON'T BELIEVE what this mom discovered!!!"
- ❌ "SHOCKING parenting hack goes VIRAL"

## The SMT Test

Before publishing any content, ask: **"Would the friend in the group chat say this?"**

If it sounds like a blog post → rewrite it.
If it sounds like a textbook → kill it.
If it sounds like every other parenting account → start over.
If it sounds like a text message you'd screenshot and forward → ship it.

## Tone by Content Pillar

### AI Magic (30%)
Tone: "Look what I just made this thing do"
Energy: Excited, slightly mischievous. Like showing someone a magic trick.
- "I asked AI to write my kid's teacher a note and... I'm never going back."
- "POV: You just saved 3 hours of meal planning in 45 seconds."

### Parenting Insights (25%)
Tone: "I just learned this and it hit different"
Energy: Reflective, real, sometimes gut-punching. Never preachy.
- "The reason your kid won't talk to you isn't attitude. It's safety."
- "Every time you say 'you're fine' to a crying kid, you're teaching them to lie about their feelings."

### Tech for Moms (20%)
Tone: "Why did nobody tell me about this app?"
Energy: Practical excitement. Lead with the result, not the tool.
- "This free app just organized my family's entire week in 30 seconds."
- "I turned my phone into a homework helper and my 4th grader thinks I'm a genius."

### Mom Health + Wellness (15%)
Tone: "We need to talk about this and nobody is"
Energy: Honest, validating, zero toxic positivity. Practical solutions only.
- "The reason you can't sleep isn't stress. It's your nervous system stuck in survival mode."
- "You're not lazy. You're running on 4 years of accumulated sleep debt."

### Trending + Culture (10%)
Tone: "Did you see this? Here's what it actually means for us"
Energy: Quick, opinionated, reframed for moms.
- "That new screen time study everyone's sharing? Here's what it actually says."
- "The internet is fighting about this parenting trend. Here's the truth."

## Language Rules

### We say:
- "your kid" (not "your child" — too formal)
- "game-changing" (when it actually is)
- "nobody's talking about this"
- "save this"
- "I tested this" / "I tried this"
- "here's the thing" / "here's what nobody tells you"

### We never say:
- "mama" or "momma" (overused, condescending)
- "self-care journey" (corporate wellness speak)
- "gentle reminder" (passive-aggressive)
- "normalize this" (virtue signaling)
- "as a mom of X kids" (credentialism)
- "hot take" (overused)
- Any variation of "you're doing great, sweetie" energy

### Emoji philosophy:
- Sparingly. Max 1-2 per caption.
- 👀 🤍 💛 — our core three. They feel knowing, warm, genuine.
- Never: 🤪 💕 ✨ 🌈 🔥 — too performative, too "momfluencer"
- Emoji as punctuation, never decoration: "they learned that from somewhere. 👀"

### Hashtag rules:
- 5-8 per post max
- Mix of niche (#momofateens) and medium (#parentinghacks)
- Never use mega-tags where you'll be invisible (#momlife, #parenting)
- Always spell-check hashtags
- Platform-specific: TikTok can go broader, IG needs more niche discovery tags

## Caption Structure

### TikTok captions: SHORT.
2-3 sentences max. The video/slideshow does the work. Caption adds the emotional landing.
- Line 1: Emotional reaction or reframe
- Line 2: One-line CTA (save, share, tag)

### Instagram captions: STORY.
Mini-essay format. 100-180 words. Feels like reading a text from your smartest friend.
- Line 1: Repeat or expand the hook (bold statement)
- Body: The insight, the reframe, the "here's what nobody tells you"
- Close: Validating line + soft CTA
- Spacing: Use line breaks. No walls of text.

## What SMT is NOT

- Not a gentle parenting account (we respect it, we don't preach it)
- Not an AI account (AI is a tool we use, not our identity)
- Not a momfluencer page (no face, no "day in my life," no hauls)
- Not a news account (we don't report — we reframe for moms)
- Not a product review account (we show results, not specs)

**SMT is the signal in the noise.** The one account moms check first because it always has something they didn't know they needed.
