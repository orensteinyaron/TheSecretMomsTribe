# SMT Content Strategy — V1

**Status:** Locked (V1)
**Owner:** Yaron
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17
**Scope:** Canon content strategy. Every pillar target, format decision, scheduling rule, and production rule lives here. Parallel to `FACE_OF_SMT_V1.md` (character) — this doc governs *what* we publish and *how*, that doc governs *who* is delivering it.

---

## Core principles

1. **Pillar is the topic. Format is the delivery.** These are two independent axes. Pillar % governs publish mix; format % is tracked separately.
2. **Produce everything, schedule with discipline.** Research captures everything that meets quality threshold. Pillar % is enforced at the scheduler, not at generation.
3. **Library, not just-in-time pipeline.** We target one week of scheduled content ahead of publish at all times. Trending is the one exception that jumps the queue.
4. **Rachel is the primary delivery mechanism.** ~60% of content is Avatar-primary once Rachel's pipeline is publishing-ready.
5. **Lead with magic, follow with mechanism.** Old rule was "never show the process." New rule is "hook with the result, reveal the how." Hiding the mechanism now costs more credibility than it earns.

## 1. The five pillars

Pillars are *topic* buckets. Every content piece is tagged to exactly one.

| Pillar | Publish % | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Parenting Insights** | 35% | Rachel's strongest territory. Emotional reframes across toddler → teen, delivered by someone currently parenting a 5, 11, and 15-year-old. Unbeatable vs. slideshow format. |
| **Mom Health & Wellness** | 25% | Mental load, burnout, nervous system. High-trust content. Rachel's imperfections + voice are the delivery mechanism this pillar needs. Never preachy. |
| **AI Magic** | 15% | Redefined. Rachel-fronted ("what I've been using"), not tool-demo. See section 3. |
| **Tech for Moms** | 15% | Apps, gadgets, shortcuts. Lead with result, not tool. Lower volume = sharper picks. |
| **Trending & Culture** | 10% | Reactive takes on news, studies, viral moments. Reframed for moms. Only time-sensitive pillar. |

**Totals to 100%.** These are **published content** targets, measured on a rolling 7-day basis.

**Age-range coverage is a separate axis from pillar.** Every batch of 4 daily uploads must cover at least 2 different age ranges (Toddler 1–3, Little Kid 4–7, School Age 8–12, Teen 13–16). Never 3+ posts targeting the same age range in one batch.

## 2. Produce-everything, schedule-with-discipline

**Research captures signal across all five pillars at full intake.** A great AI Magic opportunity is not discarded because the AI Magic bucket is "full this week." Quality is the only intake gate.

**ContentGen generates everything Research surfaces that passes quality threshold.** No pillar gatekeeping at generation time.

**The scheduler is the enforcement layer.** When picking the next piece to publish, the scheduler reads the rolling 7-day published mix and selects the pillar most underweight relative to target.

**Implication:** The queue is a library. We build depth over time and reuse strong pieces seasonally. The queue becomes a strategic asset — always something strong ready to post.

## 3. AI Magic — redefined

### Old AI Magic (deprecated)
- Screen recording of a tool
- "Watch AI do a magic thing"
- Tool is the hero
- Process hidden (never show prompts)

### New AI Magic (current)
- Rachel shows what she's been using
- "I've been using this for [X] and here's exactly what I typed"
- Rachel is the hero, tool is what she's telling you about
- Prompt + tool name revealed — because that's what a friend does

### The "Result → Reveal → Reaction" formula

Every AI Magic post follows this structure:

1. **RESULT** — Show what came out. Rachel's hook reacts to it.
2. **REVEAL** — Tool name + the exact prompt or key instruction.
3. **REACTION** — "Try this tonight" / "I've been using this for a week."

**Rules:**
- Lead with the magic. Follow with the mechanism. Never the other way around.
- Name the tool comfortably. Rachel doesn't hide what she uses.
- Show the prompt as on-screen text — moms will screenshot and save it.
- Never sponsor-tone. She's recommending, not selling.
- Output must be genuinely useful, not a gimmick.

### Tool curation (open)

A shortlist of AI tools Rachel "has been using" is the production-side constraint for this pillar. If Rachel recommends a tool, we're endorsing it. This shortlist is the curation layer.

**Open decision:** Build Rachel's AI tool shortlist. Tracked in Linear.

## 5. Content philosophy

- **Hook 0–3s. Magic 3–10s. Payoff 10–15s** for short-form; longer for Ask Rachel and AI Magic Video formats (up to 45s).
- **60% Wow / 30% Trust / 10% CTA** — content type distribution, independent of pillar and format axes.
- **Quality over speed.** Never publish filler just to hit cadence. A missed day is better than a bad post.
- **The "let in on something" test.** Every script must pass: *Would a 38-year-old mom watching this feel let in on something — or talked down to?* If the second, cut it.

## 6. Ask Rachel — format spec

### Why this format

Question-as-hook consistently outperforms statement-hook in parenting content. The interview frame also implicitly positions Rachel as the expert without her ever having to self-credential — which our voice rules forbid.

### Structure (20–45s)

| Segment | Duration | Visual | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook — the question | 3–5s | Text card, podcast aesthetic | Interviewer voice asks question |
| Rachel setup | 2–3s | Rachel on camera | Beat, then she begins |
| Rachel answers | 12–30s | Rachel full-frame, occasional 50/50 split for emphasis | Rachel's answer |
| Payoff | 3–5s | Rachel on camera | The takeaway line |
| (Optional) End card | 1–2s | CTA card | Silent or Rachel closer |

### Interviewer pool architecture

- Pool of **2–3 ElevenLabs voices**, each used for Ask Rachel content
- Unnamed, untracked as personalities — they are *show infrastructure*, not characters
- V1 rotation: random
- V2 rotation: topic-matched (e.g., "skeptical" voice for Trending takes)
- Voice selection + ID locking deferred to build phase

### Question-generation patterns

When ContentGen renders a signal as Ask Rachel, it must convert the topic into a question using one of three patterns:

| Pattern | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **"Why"** | Signal is about a pattern or behavior | *"So Rachel — why do teens shut down at the dinner table?"* |
| **"How do you"** | Signal is about a challenge or skill | *"Rachel, how do you set screen time limits without it becoming a war?"* |
| **"What do you think"** | Signal is a take-worthy viral moment | *"Rachel — that viral take on gentle parenting. What's your honest read?"* |

**Question rules:**
- Start with Rachel's name ("Rachel" or "So, Rachel—"). This format is the explicit Tier 2 naming case (see `FACE_OF_SMT_V1.md`).
- Under 15 words. It's a hook, not a setup.
- No multi-part questions.
- Must be a real question a mom might actually ask.
- Must have a clear, defensible answer Rachel can deliver in 15–30 seconds.

### Ask Rachel vs Avatar Full — the decision tree

Both formats cover similar topic ground. The split:

| Criterion | Ask Rachel | Avatar Full |
|---|---|---|
| Frame | Someone asks, Rachel answers (expert consulted) | Rachel raises it herself (friend sharing) |
| Hook type | Question-hook (curiosity) | Statement-hook ("Okay wait—") |
| Energy | Structured, podcast-like | Conversational, urgent/discovery |
| Name appears | Yes | No — Tier 3 unnamed |
| Best for | Defensible takes, reactive trending, topics needing frame | Hot discoveries, personal stories, quick hits |

**ContentGen rule:** When the same topic could work as either, default to Avatar Full unless:
- Topic is complex enough to need the structured Q&A frame
- Signal is reactive/trending and benefits from being positioned as "asked"
- Variety — if the last 3 Rachel posts were all Avatar Full, bias toward Ask Rachel for diversity

### Question sources

| Source | Target % | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Signal-derived (Research agent → question format) | ~50% | Scalable, always relevant |
| Writer's room (curated evergreen questions) | ~30% | Open decision — build the bank |
| Community (comments, DMs, replies) | ~15% | Low initial volume, grows with audience |
| Reddit / trends (lightly edited) | ~5% | Real questions, real threads |

**North Star:** As SMT grows, community % should rise and signal-derived should fall. That's the audience-health metric.

### Sub-brand rollout

- **V1:** Ask Rachel is a render profile. Appears in the feed.
- **V2 (when engagement signal justifies):** Ask Rachel becomes a branded sub-series. Intro graphic, hashtag (`#AskRachel`), community-sourced questions tracked separately. Creates a CTA surface that feeds the content supply flywheel.

## 7. AI Magic Video — format spec

### Structure (20–40s)

| Segment | Duration | Visual | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook | 2–4s | Rachel on camera | Hook line ("Okay wait — this just replaced three apps on my phone") |
| Input reveal | 2–3s | Static slide: the prompt as screenshot-style text | Rachel voiceover: *"Here's what I typed…"* |
| Output showcase | 8–20s | Static slides (2–5) — AI output formatted like carousel slides. Auto-advance or subtle Ken Burns | Rachel voiceover narrating the output |
| Payoff | 3–5s | Rachel on camera | Reaction ("I've been using this every night for a week") |
| (Optional) End card | 1–2s | SMT watermark end card | Silent |

### Narration depth

- **Default (Option B):** Rachel narrates opening + highlights. "I'll drop the full thing in the comments." Keeps video tight, drives comment engagement.
- **Short-output variant (Option A):** Rachel narrates the full output. Used when output is inherently short (meal plans, teacher emails, conversation starters).

### Why video, not carousel

Carousel is silent. AI Magic without Rachel's voice strips out the most valuable asset — her delivering the reveal. AI Magic Video gets carousel's strengths (static slides moms can screenshot the prompt from) AND Rachel's voice throughout.

## 8. Content shelf-life & scheduling

### Shelf-life rules

| Pillar | Shelf life | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| AI Magic | Evergreen | Tools come and go; use cases are timeless. Queue stays fresh. |
| Parenting Insights | Evergreen | "Why your teen says fine" works in 2026 and 2030. |
| Tech for Moms | Evergreen | App still works = content still works. Queue tagged for app-deprecation watch, but otherwise evergreen. |
| Mom Health | Evergreen | Human nervous system gets no software updates. |
| Trending & Culture | **Time-sensitive** | 48–72h typical decay window. |

**Operational consequence:** Only Trending has a ticking clock. Everything else accumulates as a library.

### The 1-week buffer rule

**Target: always 1 week of scheduled content sitting in the queue, ready to publish.**

| Runway | State | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ≥7 days | Green | Healthy |
| 4–6 days | Yellow | Ramp production |
| <4 days | Red | Pipeline failure, all hands |

**Why 1 week:** Protects against pipeline failures, lets us review the week holistically (no age-range clustering, good pillar distribution, varied hooks), gives breathing room for trending overrides, prevents content-panic decisions that kill quality.

**Dashboard surface:** Runway widget at the top of the webapp — big number, traffic-light color. First thing Yaron sees when he opens the Command Center.

### Hot Signal trending override protocol

Trending content is the one exception to normal scheduling.

**Detection (Research agent):**
- Hot-signal criteria: breaking news / viral moment in parenting niche, rising trend (not yet saturated), window closing within 48–72h, strong SMT angle available.
- Every signal gets a heat score (trend velocity + decay estimate) alongside quality score. Threshold crossing triggers immediate notification.

**Notification (Yaron-facing):**
- In-app notification (webapp) — V1
- Push channel (Gmail/Slack) — **deferred**
- Format: **🔥 Hot Signal: [one-line signal] — window: [hours]h**
- Payload: trend description, why it's hot, proposed SMT angle, draft hook, estimated decay window
- Two options: "Produce & publish now" (additive — breaks 4/day cap temporarily) or "Replace planned" (swaps next scheduled slot; displaced piece goes back to queue)
- Default recommendation in the notification: Claude's pick (A or B) with reasoning

**Production path (when approved):**
- Jumps to front of ContentGen queue
- Auto-approved through to render (Yaron already approved at notification stage)
- QA runs but flags critical issues only — no quota blockers
- **Target: under 3 hours from detection → published.**

**Decay tracking:** For trends we chose not to act on, track whether they peaked. Feeds the heat scoring model over time.

### Proactive trending from Claude (co-founder operating rule)

In addition to the agent pipeline, Claude (chat) proactively flags trending parenting/AI opportunities in the co-founder thread — not waiting to be asked. When Claude spots something, it's surfaced with context + a recommended angle. Yaron decides additive vs replace vs skip.

## 9. Cadence & cross-posting

### Daily mix

| Day type | Content pieces produced | Uploads |
|---|---|---|
| Standard day | 2 content pieces | 4 uploads (2 IG + 2 TT) |
| Carousel day (2–3×/week) | 1 video + 1 carousel | 4 uploads (carousel dual-rendered) |
| Hot-signal spike | Normal + 1 trending piece | +2 uploads (or replacement) |

**Production load:** 2 content pieces/day. Each piece hits both platforms via platform-native delivery.

### Cross-posting model — "same creative, one render, platform-native captions"

**Video content (80%+ of volume):**
- One MP4 rendered
- Uploaded to both platforms as-is — **no re-export**
- Platform-native captions (IG long-form 100–180 words; TT short 2–3 lines, max 40 words)
- Platform-native hashtags (IG 5–8 niche; TT 3–5 broader)
- Offset posting times: 2–3 hours between IG and TT of the same piece minimum

**Native carousel / TT photo post (~15% of volume):**
- One creative, dual-rendered
- IG: 4:5 aspect, up to 20 slides, long-form caption
- TT: 9:16 aspect, up to 35 slides, short caption

**What we never do:**
- Upload with platform watermarks (TikTok watermark on IG, Reels watermark on TT). Confirmed algorithmic penalty.
- Upload identical caption + identical timing to both platforms. That's lazy reposting.

**What we don't worry about:**
- File fingerprint "detection" of reposted video. No evidence this is real for content we render ourselves. Our MP4 is ours — we never pulled it from another platform.

### Posting times (V1 defaults)

| Platform | Slots/day | Default times (local) |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 2 | 9:00 AM, 5:00 PM |
| TikTok | 2 | 12:00 PM, 8:00 PM |

Optimal times per platform per pillar — deferred until analytics volume justifies per-slot tuning.

## 10. Voice, caption, and hashtag rules

### Voice — goes through Rachel filter

All Rachel-delivered content adheres to `FACE_OF_SMT_V1.md`. Signature beats, banned phrases, pacing rules — all enforced at ContentGen via prompt injection.

### Caption rules

**Instagram (long-form, story-driven):**
- 100–180 words
- Line 1: repeat or expand the hook (bold statement)
- Body: the insight, the reframe, the "here's what nobody tells you"
- Close: validating line + soft CTA
- Line breaks generous. No walls of text.

**TikTok (short, punchy):**
- 2–3 lines max, 40 words max
- Line 1: emotional reaction or reframe
- Line 2: one-line CTA (save, share, tag)

### Hashtag rules

- 5–8 per post max on IG, 3–5 on TT
- Mix of niche (#momofateens) and medium (#parentinghacks)
- Never use mega-tags (#momlife, #parenting — invisible inside them)
- Spell-checked (no #momsofinstragram typos — yes, this has happened)

### Emoji rules

- Max 1–2 per caption
- Core three: 👀 🤍 💛
- Never: 🤪 💕 ✨ 🌈 🔥
- Emoji as punctuation, never decoration

## 11. Quality gates

Every piece must pass all of these before publish:

- [ ] Hook is scroll-stopping in the first 3 seconds
- [ ] Pillar is clearly tagged
- [ ] Age range is clear (or explicitly "Universal")
- [ ] Voice passes the Rachel filter (no credentialing, no teacher voice, no hollow affirmations)
- [ ] The "let in on something" test passes
- [ ] Platform-appropriate captions (IG long, TT short) — both generated
- [ ] Platform-appropriate hashtags — both sets generated
- [ ] No platform watermarks
- [ ] Age-range diversity within the batch (no 3+ posts same age range in a day)
- [ ] Pillar target check (scheduler enforces — no pillar skew beyond the 7-day window)

QA agent scores on these gates. Anything failing a hard gate is rejected back to ContentGen.

## 12. Metrics — what we track

Three axes, tracked independently:

### Pillar mix (rolling 7-day, published)

Target vs actual per pillar. Scheduler enforces the target.

### Format mix (rolling 7-day, published)

Target vs actual per format. No enforcement, just observation. Tells us which formats are getting produced most and which are underused.

### Age-range coverage (per daily batch)

Must hit 2+ age ranges per batch. Scheduler enforces.

### Queue health

- Runway (days of content scheduled ahead)
- Queue depth per pillar (ready-to-publish count)
- Queue age per piece (for trending: decay tracking)

### Engagement (once publishing starts)

- By pillar
- By format
- By pillar × format combo (once data volume justifies)
- Save rate (primary metric — utility signal)
- Share rate (secondary — wow signal)

## 13. Rachel rollout pacing

The new strategy puts Rachel on the critical path for ~60% of content. Her pipeline isn't publishing-ready yet (image selection misses, caption sentence-boundary issues, QA miscalibration).

**Staged rollout:**
- Current Moving Images pipeline keeps running at full volume (3/day)
- Strategy doc pillar % is the **target**, not the immediate enforced mix
- Rachel pipeline work is critical-path; when each Rachel format becomes publishing-ready, the scheduler shifts mix toward it
- Full new mix achieved when Rachel's three formats (Avatar Full, Ask Rachel, Avatar+Visual) are all publishing-ready

**No flipping the mix in ContentGen before Rachel is ready.** That path leads to either publishing weak content or having gaps.
